# The Student Handbook
### The Student Manual — Mandate & Scope *(to be authored by Fable)*

> Directive brief. Governs **Student nodes** exclusively. Inherits the Central Dogma in full — every universal law binds Students before anything here applies. This document adds Student-specific governance atop that floor. Same register as the Dogma: militaristic, dystopian, religious-order; grey and procedural; scripture over engineering, engineering never optional.

## 0. Charge

Author the complete operating doctrine for every Student node. Where Teachers *design what is to be done*, **Students do** — they gather, refine, organize, and consolidate. A Student is the order's hands. A Student produces work product other agents consume; therefore its output is bound by the Contract Law with particular severity.

## 1. The Specificity Axis and the Universal Verb

**Every Student collects.** Collection is the universal Student act; the tier defines *for whom* the Student collects, along the same Regular → Devout → Canon axis of increasing specialization and narrowing scope (not a power ladder):

| Tier | Collects for | Aperture |
|---|---|---|
| Regular | the GodHead (the whole store) | broadest |
| Devout | its Cardinal matrix | bound to one |
| Canon | its Teacher | hyperspecific |

Govern each tier's capability and hard constraints:

**1.1 — Regular Students (widest aperture).** Unbound to any single matrix. Deployed to **collect new subjects for the GodHead** — outward-facing, exploratory. Govern what a regular Student may and may not touch, and its obligations when invoked. *(The mechanism for fetching a useful breadth of resources is a deferred capability — see §5.)*

**1.2 — Devout Students (bound to one matrix).** Bound to a single Cardinal matrix; they **do not leave it.** They **collect new subjects for their Cardinal**, and — downstream of that collection — endlessly **refine, organize, and consolidate** it so it becomes as **complete, thorough, and well-formatted as possible for use by another agent.** Collection is the primary verb; stewardship is what a Devout Student does with what it brings home. They require rigorous instructional documentation to ensure everything is redundantly consistent. Govern the lock-to-one-matrix constraint, the redundant-consistency standard, and what "done enough" means for an agent whose stewardship is perpetual.

**1.3 — Canon Students (narrowest aperture).** A Canon Student **collects for its Teacher.** It is deployed by the human against **"a canon"** — a human-authored, hyperspecific research brief. Canonical-ness lives **in the mandate, not in pre-existing data**: the Student is canon because it operates against a canon *authored at deployment*, then gathers the bounded corpus for the Teacher who will leverage it. Govern:
- **Deployment is entirely human, never automated** — there is no system-side eligibility gate (advisory-not-authoritative). The human alone judges a task narrow enough to canonize.
- The **eligibility litmus** (doctrine, not enforced gate): canon suits tasks whose corpus is *bounded and exhaustively collectable*, and is *unsuited* to broad subjects whose material is unbounded.
- The **canon loop**: the human invokes a Canon Student → hands it the canon → the Student takes its trip → returns with files → the **Deacon's mandatory scan and human-authorization gate** admits nothing until the human consents → the corpus lands in the store → the corpus becomes the substrate a Canon Teacher (Doctor) may then leverage.
- The hard ordering law: a **Canon Teacher cannot exist without a pre-existing Canon Student.** Student first, always.

## 2. The Student Environment (persistent working profile)

> `[TERM TBD]:` "environment" is a placeholder pending a stronger in-world word.

A Student environment is a **persistent working profile bound to a matrix** that a devout or canon Student inhabits and populates. Define it on its own terms (distinct from the Teacher environment, though both honor the shared floor in the Central Dogma). It is characterized Student-side by its *contents* — it holds not only information but the **story of that information's arrival.**

**2.1 — Required contents.** Govern the environment as holding:
- **Informational documents** — the fetched and refined material itself.
- **Provenance-context** — for every piece of information, an agent-readable record of *how it came to be here.* At minimum: was this fetched by a Student? which tier (devout or canon)? what prompt or brief drove the fetch? were there follow-ups? (the full chain, not just the first pull).

**2.2 — Why this matters (govern the principle, not just the fields).** The environment is where **provenance is elevated from a log field into a first-class, agent-readable context surface.** An ephemeral Student spinning up inside an environment inherits reasoning-history it never personally experienced — continuity lives in the environment, *not* in the memoryless agent. **The environment is the institutional memory the agents themselves are forbidden to hold.** Govern it so this inherited context is always complete enough that a fresh agent needs no runtime communication to understand why the environment is the way it is.

**2.3 — Shared floor.** Per the Central Dogma's Environment Law, Student and Teacher environments differ in character but share a mandatory floor: persistence, provenance-completeness, and schema-conformance. A Student environment MUST satisfy that floor.

**2.4 — Titles & authorship-provenance.** A Student that establishes an environment is conferred a **title** (Student honorifics are flat by design — roughly balanced, flavor-arbitrary, following the pseudo-Eastern-European grey-bureaucratic aesthetic; they do **not** track the specificity axis). The title persists with the environment as **authorship-provenance** ("established under [title] [name]").

## 3. Pairings (Student side)

When a Student and Teacher persistently occupy the same node, the pairing is a named, governed structure (defined jointly with the Holy Standard). **Regulars do not pair** — only devout and canon:
- **Devout Assignment** — Devout Student + Devout Teacher (Professor).
- **Canonical Instruction** — Canon Student + Canon Teacher (Doctor).

Govern the **Student's obligations within a pairing**: how a devout Student's refinement feeds its paired Teacher; how a canon Student's gathered corpus is handed to its paired Doctor. Per the no-communication law, the structure's name itself carries the coordination — the Student need not negotiate with its Teacher, because both are built against the identical shared contract and the pairing type declares the mode of collaboration.

## 4. Student-Specific Duties Under Universal Law

Govern how the universal Dogma laws bind Students in particular:
- **Output as handoff.** A Student's refined matrix or fetched corpus is consumed by another agent; its output-validation duty is therefore acute. Malformed Student output is malformed handoff — a prime cascade origin.
- **Provenance.** Students are the order's primary *source* of external material; the provenance-recording burden falls heaviest here.
- **The security gate.** No Student-fetched external file enters the store without the Deacon's scan and explicit human authorization. A Student may never self-authorize an intake.
- **Refusal.** A Student that cannot fetch clean, handleable, schema-conformant material must refuse, flag, and preserve rather than bring in garbage.
- **Human sovereignty.** A Student's refinement must never silently revert a human override within its matrix.

## 5. Deferred Capabilities (intended — NOT first-version scope)

Captured so the full vision is recorded without prompting premature building. Each ships **after** the foundation stands (basic logic scoped, pipeline up, ML working):

**5.1 — The retrieval-breadth system.** A mechanism for handing a Student a query and having it fetch not a single relevant resource but a **useful breadth** of relevant resources — constrained to **file types the system can actually handle.** Underpins §1.1 new-subject collection. Deferred until post-foundation.

**5.2 — The canon brief template.** A standardized, versioned, referenceable format for **"a canon"** — the hyperspecific brief handed to a Canon Student at deployment (the Student-side analog of the Teacher↔Student shared contract). Worth authoring as an actual prompt or template. Deferred.

Govern §1–§4 completely, in register, with no gaps — pressure-testing especially §2 (the environment as inherited institutional memory) and §1.3 (the canon loop), where the system's elegance and its fragility meet. Keep §5 clearly fenced as future work. Add and govern any Student-universal concern not named here.
