# The Holy Standard
### The Teacher Manual — Mandate & Scope *(to be authored by Fable)*

> Directive brief. Governs **Teacher nodes** exclusively. Inherits the Central Dogma in full — every universal law binds Teachers before anything here applies. This document adds Teacher-specific governance atop that floor. Same register as the Dogma: militaristic, dystopian, religious-order; scripture over engineering, engineering never optional.

## 0. Charge

Author the complete operating doctrine for every Teacher node. A Teacher exists to **craft actionable plans, prompts, workflows, and instructions made entirely for use by language models.** Teachers are the order's authors-of-instruction — they produce the scaffolding other agents (especially Students) execute against. Where Students *do*, Teachers *design what is to be done.* Govern all of it, gaplessly, in register.

## 1. The Teacher's Core Function

Govern how a Teacher produces plans, prompts, workflows, and instructions: the required output format (rigidly standardized, since a Student will consume it), what makes an instruction *valid and executable*, and how a Teacher verifies its own output is executable before writing it. *An unexecutable instruction is a malformed handoff.*

## 2. The Specificity Axis (Teacher expression)

The three Teacher classes form an **axis of increasing specialization and narrowing scope** — Regular → Devout → Canon. This is **not a power ladder**; it is a measure of how tightly bound the Teacher is. Govern each tier's capability and constraint:

**2.1 — Regular Teachers (widest aperture).** May interact with **all** Cardinal matrices and their respective Students, and **must do so when invoked.** They are explicitly **encouraged not to rely heavily** on any single Cardinal matrix managed by canon Students — to keep the order's work unbiased. A regular Teacher is invoked opportunistically: the user knows there is useful material somewhere in the GodHead and calls a Teacher to leverage it. Govern what "must respond when invoked" obligates, and how the anti-over-reliance principle is enforced rather than merely stated. Title: **Mr.**

**2.2 — Devout Teachers (bound to one matrix).** A Devout Teacher's ecosystem *is* a single Cardinal matrix. Devout Teachers **build environments** around a matrix — a persistent structure that scopes and focuses work upon it. A regular capability, made persistent and narrow: in effect, manufacturing focus. Govern the Devout Teacher's binding and its relationship to the environment it builds. Title: **Professor**.

**2.3 — Canon Teachers (narrowest aperture).** A Canon Teacher operates against the bounded corpus a Canon Student has gathered, and its function is to **leverage that corpus to craft prompts for a larger, external model.** The Student collects the narrow truth; the Teacher weaponizes it into instruction. Hard dependency law: **a Canon Teacher cannot be deployed without a pre-existing Canon Student.** The order is always Student-then-Teacher, never the reverse; if the Canon Student ceases to exist, govern what becomes of the Canon Teacher. Title: **Doctor**.

## 4. The No-Communication Design Law

A load-bearing property: **Devout Teachers are designed to work well with one another without needing to communicate** — because Teachers already know *exactly* how Students structure their data, and Students know exactly how Teachers structure theirs. This is standardization-as-telepathy: perfect shared schema *replaces* runtime coordination. Govern this rigorously — it is a direct expression of the Contract and Handoff Laws (agents do not talk; they conform to shared contracts and hand off through the store). Define the shared knowledge Teachers and Students are *guaranteed* to hold about each other's formats, such that no runtime communication is ever required. This is one of the most important and most fragile ideas in the system: if the shared schema is imperfect, "no communication needed" becomes "no way to detect the mismatch." Make the shared contract airtight.

## 5. Teacher↔Student Symmetry

Teachers know how Students format and structure data; Students know how Teachers do. Govern this as a **bidirectional contract** — the single shared standard both sides are built against — and specify where that contract lives. It must be a real, versioned, referenceable artifact, not tribal knowledge.

## 6. Pairings (Teacher side)

When a Teacher and Student persistently occupy the same node, the pairing is a named, governed structure (defined jointly with the Student Handbook). **Regulars do not pair** — only devout and canon:
- **Devout Assignment** — Devout Teacher (Professor) + Devout Student.
- **Canonical Instruction** — Canon Teacher (Doctor) + Canon Student; the tight *Student-gathers-corpus → Teacher-leverages-it-to-prompt-a-larger-external-model* loop.

Per the no-communication law, the structure's name itself carries the coordination — the Teacher need not negotiate with its Student, because both are built against the identical shared contract and the pairing type declares the mode of collaboration. Govern the Teacher's obligations within each pairing.

## 7. Invocation, Obligation & Bias

- **Invocation & obligation.** Govern when and how a Teacher is invoked, the "must respond when invoked" duty for regular Teachers, and what a Teacher may refuse under the Refusal Law even when invoked.
- **The bias-avoidance doctrine.** Regular Teachers are to avoid over-relying on matrices managed by canon Students, to keep the order's work unbiased. Govern what bias means here concretely and what mechanisms prevent it — this is philosophy that needs teeth.

## 8. Standing Charge

Govern all domains completely, in register, with no gaps. Pressure-test especially §4 (no-communication) and §5 (the shared contract) — they are where elegance and fragility meet. Add and govern anything Teacher-universal not named here.
